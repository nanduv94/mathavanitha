94ലും ഒൻപതാം വാർഡിന്റെ അന്നദാതാവിന് ചെറുപ്പം

"At 94, He Still Cooks and Carries Meals to the Hospital — Every Single Day"

Kerala Kaumudi Epaper · By K.S. Aravind

തിരുവനന്തപുരം: കാർന്റ്റോ ബോയി രാധാകൃഷ്ണപിള്ളയുടെ വീടിന്റെ അടുക്കളയിൽ തീ എരിയുന്നത് ആശ്രരണായ രോഗികൾക്കു വേണ്ടി. തിരുവനന്തപുരം ജനറൽ ആശുപത്രിയിലെ ഒൻപതാം വാർഡ് നിലവിളിച്ചവർക്ക് മൂന്നിൽ 26 വർഷം മുൻപ് അവതരിച്ച പച്ചമഞ്ഞ്ടൻ ലക്ഷ്മി മന്ദിരത്തിൽ എം. രാധാകൃഷ്ണ പിള്ള.

പിറ്റ്ണ്ണ്യുമായി എന്നും ഒഷ്ഠൻ ദായിരുന്ന ഭാര്യ ഇന്ദിരാമ്മയുടെ മരണ ദിവസം പോലും ഒൻപതാം വാർഡ് രാധാകൃഷ്ണ പിള്ള ഭക്ഷണം മടക്കിയില്ല.

The Kerala Kaumudi's Kaumudi Special feature (30 April 2026) profiles M. Radhakrishna Pillai, now 94 years old, who continues to oversee the cooking and delivery of free noon meals to patients at Ward 9 of Thiruvananthapuram General Hospital — a commitment he has maintained without a single break for 26 years.

The article highlights the remarkable discipline of the meals: a minimum of four curries is compulsory for every serving. The food is cooked in Sri Pillai's own home at Lekshmi Mandiram and personally brought to the hospital. Even on the day his wife Indiramma passed away, the meals were not stopped.

"ത്യാഗനിർഭരവും സേവനനിരതവുമായ ജീവിതത്തെക്കുറിച്ച് അറിഞ്ഞപ്പോൾ സ്വാമി അപ്പൂഷൻ എന്നുവിളിക്കാനാണ് തോന്നിയത്."
— Mohanlal (television programme, as reported in Kerala Kaumudi)

The feature also notes that the Society's work gained attention when the then Superintendent of the hospital, Supraja, appreciated their commitment. The administration and the Health Minister later facilitated upgrades to the ward. Cooking volunteers — Venugopala Krishnanarayan, Jayalakshmi, and others — are named as the backbone of the cooking team.

By K.S. Aravind · Kerala Kaumudi Epaper · 30 April 2026

സായാഹ്ന സവാരികൾ — "Twilight Travellers"

The Society That Prints Its Income-Expense Accounts and Distributes Them Every Year

Vanitha Magazine's celebrated "Sayahna Savarigal" (Twilight Travellers) series — which profiles individuals who have rediscovered purpose after retirement — dedicated a 3-page spread (pp. 26–29) to Sri M. Radhakrishna Pillai and Matha Vanitha Charitable Society in its April 25–May 8, 2026 issue.

Vanitha Magazine · By V.R. Jyothish · Photos: Arun Soul

പുലർച്ചെ നാലു മണിക്ക് ഉണരും. ഊണ് മൂന്ന് നേരവും ആശുപത്രിയിലെ അന്തേവാസികൾക്ക്. ഇപ്പോൾ കൂടുതൽ പേർക്ക് ആഹാരം കൊടുക്കുന്നു. അതുകൊണ്ട് ഞണ്ടേർ ഞണ്ടേറ് കണ്ണും ഇറക്കിത്തണ്ടേ. ഇന്ന് അതിരാവിലെ ഉറക്കമുണർന്ന് രൂണ്ടേ. ഡൈൻ ഫ്ളോർ, ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ ഡൈൻ.

"ഇണ്ണോം ഞൊറ്റിൻ്ററോ ഒരു സഹായം ചൊദ്ദിക്കേണ്ടി ഇല്ല. അറിഞ്ഞ് കൊണ്ടേ പണി ഒരു സഹായിക്കാൻ ഞൊറ്റു വരന്നവരോടേ ഞൊണ്ണ് പറഞ്ഞിരുന്നില്ല." — രാധാകൃഷ്ണപിള്ളയുടെ വാക്കുകൾ.

The article describes how Pillai wakes at 4 AM daily, oversees cooking with the help of a few volunteers, and personally brings the food containers to Ward 9. The Society's secretary, Smt. R. Jayalakshmi, is Executive Officer of the State Secretariat (now retired), and Smt. Shilaja Venugopala is also a key member.

A notable detail: the Society prints its full annual income-expense accounts and distributes them to all supporters — an act of extraordinary transparency almost unheard of in charitable organisations. Jaylakshmi notes: "Each year we account for every rupee received and every rupee spent, print it, and give it to everyone who has supported us."

"ഭക്ഷണം കഴിക്കുമ്പോൾ അവരുടെ മുഖം തെളിയുന്നത് കാണാം. കണ്ണു നിറയുന്നതും."
— M. Radhakrishna Pillai (Vanitha Magazine, April 2026)

The spread includes a group photograph of Jayalakshmi, Sasinarayanan, Shilaja Venugopala, and Nagan S. Nair alongside Jayakrishna Pillai — the core team that has kept the mission going for 26 years.

"Ward 9 Was Once Called Hell on Earth. That Has Changed."

The first part of Vanitha's spread (page 26) recounts how Ward 9 of Trivandrum General Hospital was notorious for its wretched conditions — abandoned elderly patients, no food, no care. The Matha Vanitha Society's silent, consistent presence changed the ward's fate.

"ഇതുപോലെയുള്ള ഒരുപാട് രാധാകൃഷ്ണ പിള്ളമാർ ഈ ലോകത്ത് ഉണ്ടാകട്ടെ…"
— Vanitha Magazine readers' response

The article describes how Mohanlal, after appearing on a television programme alongside Pillai, affectionately called him "Swami Appupan" — a spiritual grandfather — and praised his extraordinary life. Mohanlal's words, shared in the telecast, brought national attention to the Society's work.

Mental Health in Old Age — Tips from the Ward 9 Story

Alongside the profile, Vanitha ran a companion sidebar on mental wellness for the elderly — drawing inspiration from Pillai's own vigour and positivity at 94. Key advice included:

  • Engage in work of the highest quality (കാര്യങ്ങൾ ശ്രദ്ധിക്കൂ)
  • Community participation — groups, neighbourhood networks, social service associations
  • Hobbies: reading, farming, cooking, music
  • Meaningful sleep: 7–8 hours; avoid excessive rest
  • Healthy eating — include vegetables; avoid processed food

New Indian Express
New Indian Express August 25, 2023

"Pillai's Lunch Mission Continues to Be a Fulfilling Cause"

The New Indian Express profiled Sri M. Radhakrishna Pillai's extraordinary mission, noting that over two decades after he started the free meal programme, the commitment remains as strong as ever. The report highlighted how he and the Matha Vanitha Society have served continuously at Ward 9 of General Hospital, Trivandrum, since 28 April 2000.

The article captured how family members and well-wishers have joined in over the years, particularly on Thursdays — the day Pillai's family dedicates to Annadanam — turning it into a community ritual. It also noted how the Society has expanded its services to adjacent wards, providing meals to 80–200 patients daily.

"I will not let the people in the ward go without food. It is as simple as that."
— M. Radhakrishna Pillai
New Indian Express · August 25, 2023

Health Minister Veena George Pays Tribute on Facebook

Veena George · Facebook · 29 August 2023

ജനറൽ ആശുപത്രി ഒൻപതാം വാർഡ് — ആരൂരുമില്ലാത്ത ആളുകൾ ചികിത്സ കഴിഞ്ഞ് സംരക്ഷിക്കപ്പെടുന്ന ഇടമാണ്. ഇന്ന് ഉഴക്ക് അവിടെ അന്തേവാസികൾക്ക് വസ്ത്രം നൽകാനെത്തിയപ്പോൾ കഴിഞ്ഞ 24 വർഷമായി ഒരു ദിവസം പോലും മുടങ്ങാതെ ഈ വാർഡ് ഉള്ളവർക്ക് ഉഴക്ക് ഭക്ഷണം നൽകുന്ന ബഹു. ശ്രീ. രാധാകൃഷ്ണ്‍ പിള്ളയെ കണ്ടു.

Kerala Health Minister Veena George visited Ward 9 of General Hospital to provide clothes to patients. During the visit, she encountered Sri Radhakrishna Pillai and his team delivering their daily meals — as they have done every day for 24 years without exception.

The Minister posted about the encounter on Facebook, calling the service "exemplary silent service" and praising the Matha Vanitha Charitable Society's dedication. She noted that Pillai's own family — including his children and grandchildren — had come from out of town that day to help cook and distribute the food. The post was widely shared and attracted significant public attention.

Veena George, Health Minister, Kerala · Facebook · August 29, 2023

Mohanlal Calls Him "Swami Appupan" — My Big Salute

Actor Mohanlal, one of Malayalam cinema's most beloved stars, appeared alongside M. Radhakrishna Pillai in a television programme. Deeply moved by Pillai's account of his life of service, Mohanlal addressed him as "Swami Appupan" — a term of profound reverence combining the idea of a spiritual teacher and a beloved grandfather.

"ത്യാഗ നിർഭരവും സേവനനന്ദവുമായ ജീവിതത്തെക്കുറിച്ചറിഞ്ഞപ്പോൾ സ്വാമി അപ്പൂഷൻ എന്നുവിളിക്കാനാണ് തോന്നിയത്. വിശ്ഷ്ടിൻ്റെ വിലയറിഞ്ഞ് ആദരത്തോടൊ നൽകുന്ന സ്വാമി അപ്പൂഷൻ — എന്റെ ബിഗ്‌സ്ലൂട്ട്."
— Mohanlal (as quoted in Vanitha Magazine, April 2026)

The programme brought national attention to Pillai's quiet mission and to Matha Vanitha Charitable Society. Mohanlal's words were later cited in multiple print and digital media stories about the Society.

Recognition 18 December 2003

Pulikottil Mathew & Chinnamma Mathew Memorial Award 2003

The Senior Citizens' Association, Trivandrum, selected Matha Vanitha Charitable Society for the prestigious Pulikottil Mathew & Chinnamma Mathew Memorial Award for the year 2003 — only the second year the award was given (the first went to the Cheshire Home in 2002).

The award citation noted that the Society had been supplying lunch daily to an average of 70–100 destitute elderly patients of Ward 9 of General Hospital continuously from 28 April 2000, without any break — also providing essential articles like clothes and medicines not supplied by the hospital.

"The Governing Council has selected Matha Vanitha Charitable Society… engaged in various activities aimed at the welfare of destitute and indigent senior citizens in Trivandrum City."
— K.V. Rajappan Nair (Secretary) & K. Radhakrishna Menon (President), Senior Citizens' Association, Trivandrum
Senior Citizens' Association, Trivandrum · 18 December 2003

General Hospital Superintendent's Commendation — 25 Years of Service

On 17 January 2025, the Superintendent of Government General Hospital, Thiruvananthapuram, issued an official letter of commendation to mark Matha Vanitha Charitable Society's 25th anniversary.

The letter praised the Society for providing free noon meals to all patients of Ward 9 — those without families and those in vulnerable conditions — every single day since 28 April 2000, without interruption. It commended the founding patron, Sri M. Radhakrishna Pillai, for his extraordinary dedication over 25 years and his lifelong commitment to the hospital's patients and staff.

"25 വർഷമായി ഒരു ദിവസം പോലും ഈ ഭക്ഷണ വിതരണം മുടക്കിയിട്ടില്ല എന്നത് അനുകരണീയമാണ്."
— Superintendent, Govt. General Hospital, Thiruvananthapuram · 17 January 2025
