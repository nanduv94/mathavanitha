25 Years of Silent Service

The story of Matha Vanitha Charitable Society — born from one man's compassion and carried forward by a community's love.

How It All Began

It was on the 1st of March 2000 that a group of women joined together and started functioning by registering a society under the name "Matha Vanitha Charitable Society" near Melamkottu Amman Kovil, Pettah, Thiruvananthapuram — with Sri M. Radhakrishna Pillai, retired Superintendent of the Kerala State Technical Education Department, as patron and advisor.

The society visited Government hospitals and old-age homes in Trivandrum to find destitute patients in the lowest strata lying in the verandahs, to improve their condition. The 9th Ward of the General Hospital was selected. With this, Matha Vanitha Charitable Society extended the experience of hope to the helpless patients of that ward.

Even before the media raised a hue and cry about the pathetic situation of inmates of Ward 9, Sri Radhakrishna Pillai had already started his silent services there. Realising that quenching hunger was the basic necessity of the inmates, he — with his meagre pension — started feeding them. Gradually, relatives and friends joined hands. Not only food, but also clothes and medicines were provided on occasions.

The Ward That Was Called "Hell on Earth"

The 9th Ward of that time was a synonym of distress. Destitute elderly patients with no one to care for them, their mental anguish, the loss of their bodies and minds — helpless people had been abandoned there. A few months after the society members started providing food, reports about the pathetic situation of Ward 9 appeared in the press. Thereafter, the ward was improved by the hospital authorities and government thanks to the relief measures undertaken.

Today, that same ward which was once called "bhoomileyile naragam" (hell on earth) has been transformed. The society's unbroken presence there is a major reason for that change.

A Milestone Timeline

2000
Society registered on 1 March 2000. First meal served at Ward 9, General Hospital on 28 April 2000. Not a single day has been missed since.
2003
Received the Pulikottil Mathew & Chinnamma Mathew Memorial Award from the Senior Citizens' Association, Trivandrum — in recognition of commendable services to poor and destitute senior citizens.
2015
Extended services to include support for differently-abled individuals, those with serious illnesses, and monthly rice distribution to 15+ families.
2019
Sri K. Mahadevan, long-time volunteer and supporter, received the Sevararatna Award for his decades of contribution alongside the society.
2023
Celebrated 25 years of service. Featured in Kerala Kaumudi (30 Apr 2026), Vanitha Magazine, and praised by Kerala Health Minister Veena George via Facebook. Actor Mohanlal shared his admiration in a television programme.

The People Behind It

Sri M. Radhakrishna Pillai is the patron and founder. At 94, he still oversees the cooking and delivery of meals personally from his home at Lekshmi Mandiram, Kabarady Road. His wife, the late Smt. N.T. Indiramma (d. 26 June 2005), was joint secretary and co-founder.

Smt. R. Jayalakshmi serves as Secretary, and Sri George Joseph as Treasurer. The executive committee and the wider circle of volunteers have kept this flame burning through floods, COVID, and every other adversity.

The society accepts no donations from foreign citizens or organisations. All expenses are met from voluntary contributions by members and like-minded people. You will not see society members going around with receipt books or seeking donations.
